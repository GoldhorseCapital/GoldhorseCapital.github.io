Fixed Coupon Note (FCN) Product Specification

Issuer: Barclays

ISIN: XS3078776609

Currency: USD

Total Issue Size: USD 460,000

Minimum Investment (Denomination): USD 46,000

Key Terms

  • Tenor: 6 months
  • Trade Date: July 31, 2025
  • Issue Date: August 7, 2025
  • Final Valuation Date: February 9, 2026

Underlying Stocks

The note’s performance is linked to the worst-performing of:

  • SNAP UN Equity: Initial price = USD 9.4800
  • MRVL UW Equity: Initial price = USD 82.7100

Coupon Payments

  • Annual Interest Rate: 12.07%
  • Periodic Coupon Rate: 1.0059% (paid in USD)
  • Coupon Payment Dates:
    • September 10, 2025

    • January 9, 2026
    • February 11, 2026
  • Example: For USD 46,000 invested, each coupon = USD 462.71

Knock-Out Feature (Early Termination)

  • Knock-Out Price Level: 88% of initial prices
  • Observation: Period End
  • Memory Effect: Enabled if "True"
  • Outcome: If triggered, receive principal (USD 46,000) + accrued coupons.

Maturity Payout Conditions

If no knock-out occurs:

  • On February 9, 2026, the payout depends on whether a Knock-In event occurred:
    • No Knock-In Event: Receive full principal (USD 46,000) + final coupon, regardless of the worst-performing stock’s price.
    • Knock-In Event Occurred:
      • For AKI: A stock falls below 55% anytime during the term.
        • If worst-performing stock ≥ 80% at maturity → Receive full principal + final coupon.
        • If worst-performing stock < 80% at maturity → Receive shares of the worst-performing stock (number of shares = USD 46,000 ÷ final stock price in USD).
      • For EKI: The worst-performing stock is below 55% on February 9, 2026, which also means it is below 80% (since KI level < Strike level).
        • Receive shares of the worst-performing stock (number of shares = USD 46,000 ÷ final stock price in USD).

Knock-In Risk

  • Knock-In Price Level: 55.00%
  • Knock-In Type: European
  • Note: If "N/A," no knock-in risk exists, ensuring principal is returned in cash. For AKI, a knock-in can occur anytime; for EKI, a knock-in occurs only if the worst-performing stock is below the knock-in level on the final valuation date, implying it’s also below the strike price, leading to share delivery.

Key Risks

  • Market Risk: If a Knock-In event occurs and (for AKI) the worst-performing stock is below 80% at maturity, or (for EKI) the worst-performing stock is below 55% at maturity, you may receive shares worth less than your principal.
  • Early Termination Risk: Knock-out may limit total interest earned.
  • Knock-In Risk: With AKI, a knock-in can occur anytime; with EKI, only at maturity if the worst-performing stock is below the knock-in level.
  • Currency Risk: Exchange rate fluctuations may affect share value if delivered.
  • Issuer Risk: Barclays' creditworthiness impacts repayment.
